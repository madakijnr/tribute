Professor Albert Chinua Achebe

One the first prolific writers in the history of Nigeria, an outstanding individual in the field of literature.

Professor Albert Chinualumogu Achebe

Here is a time line of professor Chinua Achebe's life:

  • 1930 - Was born on November 16th at Ogidi in the eastern part of Nigeria
  • 1936 - Achebe entered St Philip's Central School in the Akpakaogwe region of Ogidi for his primary education.
  • 1948 - Got addmitted into the first university in Nigeria, now known as The university of Ibadan
  • 1950 - Achebe's debut as an author was when he wrote a piece for the University Herald, the university's magazine, entitled "Polar Undergraduate".
  • 1951 - He was appointed as Herald's editor during the 1951-1952 school year. He wrote is first short story titled "In a Village Church",an amusing look at the Igbo synthesis between life in rural Nigeria with Christian institutions and icons.
  • 1953 - Achebe graduted with a second-class degree honours from the university.
  • 1956 - Achebe was selected to attend the staff training school for the BBC .His first trip outside Nigeria was an opportunity to advance his technical production skills
  • 1958Achebe's most famous novel "Things Fall Apart" was published
  • 1960 - Achebe was awarded a Rockefeller Fellowship for six months of travel, which he called "the first important perk of my writing career".
  • 1961 - Achebe was promoted at the NBS to the position of Director of External Broadcasting
  • 1961 - Achebe got married to Christiana Chinwe Okoli, a lady he started dating in 1958. on the 10 September 1961
  • 1962 - Achebe became a father for the first time in his life as he welcomed his first child. His wife gave birth girl whom they named Chinelo. They later had two more kids, both were boys, Ikechukwu(1964) and Chidi(1967).
  • 1990 - Achebe was riding in a car to Lagos when an axle collapsed and the car flipped. His son Ikechukwu and the driver suffered minor injuries, but the weight of the vehicle fell on Achebe and his spine was severely damaged.
  • 2007 - Achebe was awarded the Man Booker International Prize
  • 2013 - Achebe died after a short illness on 21 March 2013 in Boston, United States. He was buried in his hometown of Ogidi, he was aged 82.

When we gather together in the moonlit village ground it is not because of the moon. Every man can see it in his own compound. We come together because it is good for kinsmen to do so.”

-- Chinua Achebe --
